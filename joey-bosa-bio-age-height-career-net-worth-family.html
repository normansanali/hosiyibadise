Joey Bosa career

NFL analyst Daniel Jeremiah projected Bosa to be among the top 10 picks in the 2016 NFL Draft in March of 2016. As one of the best prospects in the forthcoming draft, Bosa was invited to the NFL combine.

In addition to participating in positional exercises, he finished all of the necessary combined drills. At the combine, Bosa conducted interviews with 13 NFL teams, including the Tennessee Titans, Dallas Cowboys, Baltimore Ravens, New Orleans Saints, and Cleveland Browns.

Scouts found his combined performance to be rather strong overall. The San Diego Chargers selected Bosa with the third overall pick in the first round of the draft.

He was selected in the first round of the 2016 NFL Draft, ahead of Ezekiel Elliott, Taylor Decker, Eli Apple, and Darron Lee, as the five Ohio State players selected.

Bosa has worn cleats for the National Football League’s My Cause My Cleats campaign, which supports research into pediatric cancer. The cleats include #MoreThan4 to promote the idea that pediatric cancer research should get more than 4% of the National Cancer Institute’s entire annual budget.

During the National Football League’s Emotional Health Awareness Month campaign in May 2021, Bosa discussed how he came to prioritize his physical and emotional health.

Bosa’s hamstring injury kept him out of action for the first four weeks of the season. Bosa made his regular season debut on October 9 against the Oakland Raiders, a rival team in the AFC West. Bosa finished the game with two sacks, a hit, and four hurries despite only playing 27 snaps.

Bosa had a strong second season after an outstanding rookie campaign that started when the Chargers relocated from San Diego to Los Angeles. With 70 tackles and 12.5 sacks, which ranks seventh in the NFL, Bosa and Melvin Ingram together constituted one of the top pass-rushing tandems in the game.

Bosa received his first Pro Bowl selection on December 19, 2017. On the list of the NFL Top 100 Players of 2018, he was ranked #37 by his teammates.

Bosa missed all of the 2018 season until Week 11 due to a foot ailment. He finished with 5.5 sacks, 23 total tackles, nine quarterback hits, and one fumble recovery in seven games.

In the Wild Card Round, he faced the Baltimore Ravens and made his playoff debut. He recorded one sack, two tackles, and one quarterback hit in the 23-17 victory.

Bosa changed his jersey number from 99 to 97 on April 12, 2019. During his time at Ohio State, he wore 97. The Chargers exercised Bosa’s contract’s fifth-year option on April 23, 2019.

After agreeing to a five-year, $135 million contract extension with the Chargers on July 28, 2020, Bosa became the highest-paid defensive player in the NFL.

Bosa had three sacks on Josh Allen in Week 12’s game against the Buffalo Bills, and he also recovered a fumble that Allen had lost in the 27-17 defeat. His third Pro Bowl nomination was earned. He was placed 32nd on the NFL Top 100 Players of 2021 list by his fellow players.

Bosa recorded a career-high seven forced fumbles in the 2021 campaign. The 2021 season’s best single game for solo tackles for Bosa came in the Baltimore Ravens defeat on October 17, 2021. In the Chargers’ loss to the Ravens, Bosa recorded five solo tackles.

Bosa left the game against the Jacksonville Jaguars in Week 3 due to a groin issue. It was then discovered that he required surgery to restore the groin’s central muscular area.
