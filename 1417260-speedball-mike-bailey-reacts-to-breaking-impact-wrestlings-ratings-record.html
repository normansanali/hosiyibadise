Speedball Mike Bailey Reacts To Breaking IMPACT Wrestlings Ratings Record

Last week was one of IMPACT Wrestling’s major pay-per-views, Bound for Glory. One of the marquee matches was the first-time-ever bout between Will Ospreay and Speedball Mike Bailey.

Both wrestlers did not seem to have any animosity toward each other prior to this match announcement. One thing that Speedball was determined to prove was to show the wrestling world that he could match the skill set of Will Ospreay.

The duo put on an incredible show for the fans. After wrestling each other for over 17 minutes. Following the match, Bailey took to Twitter to share his gratitude. He also mentioned that while he may have came up short, he certainly proved to the world that he can square up to the best of the best.

“Thank you all so much. I proved once again that I belong at the very top – I did come up short again and I’m a little tired of coming up short. But I’m super proud of the wrestler I’ve become, where I am now, and grateful for all the support. #BoundForGlory @IMPACTWRESTLING,” Speedball Mike Bailey tweeted.

TNA is back

Recently, Wrestling Observer’s Dave Meltzer appreciated the match as a whole. He mentioned that both stars put on a show that got the fans ‘going nuts’. He then ranked the match 5.25 stars.

Bailey reacted to Meltzer’s ranking.

“Getting 5.25⭐ for my match at BFG is honestly pretty cool! We are the 1st match in Impact to get this high of a rating since Joe/AJ/Daniels which feels insane. It’s all very subjective but the hope is that people continue to pay more & more attention to TNA ’cause WE’RE BACK!”

The previous record was 5 stars earned by the Christopher Daniels vs. Samoa Joe vs. AJ Styles match at TNA Unbreakable on September 11, 2005.

This rating is one of IMPACT Wrestling’s highest-rated matches.
