Paul George Basketball Career

High School Basketball

Firstly, George started the JV team season as a junior but was quickly elevated to the varsity team once the season finished.

Furthermore, during his junior year, he was the only non-senior starter on the varsity squad.

Therefore, George began to garner attention in 2007 when he competed in the AAU tournaments.

Additionally, his coach gave him the lead position in his senior season after putting up an outstanding display at the tournaments. 

As a result, George led the Knights to the Golden League crown.

He was voted Golden League Most Valuable Player, Antelope Press Player of the Year, and a Daily News All-Area Boys’ Basketball Team member in 2007–2008.

Despite all their accomplishments, universities did not consider George a significant prospect.

Rivals.com graded him as a three-star prospect and ranked him 20th in a California recruiting class. 

 Additionally, George verbally committed to Santa Clara, the first school to give him a scholarship, but later de-committed after his high school coach advised him to keep his options open.

After a positive experience at Teiosha’s Midnight Madness event in Pepperdine, he committed to Pepperdine. 

Despite offers from Georgetown and Penn State, he preferred Fresno State University because of the more significant playing time opportunities.

College Career

George attended Fresno State for two years. 

Despite losing his second game, George made an impact with his one-handed slam dunk over Mickey McConnell, winning him SportsCenter’s top “Play of the Day” title for the date of the Year 2018, November 18. 

Additionally, Fresno State failed to qualify for the NCAA tournament that season.

George finished second in the Western Athletic Conference (WAC) in minutes played, 3-point shooting, and steals per game. 

George had the third-best 3-point field goal average in Fresno State during the Bulldogs men’s basketball program. 

In his sophomore year, George was named the most entertaining player in the west region.

On March 31, 2010, George revealed his intention to enter the 2010 NBA Draft, forgoing his remaining two years of college eligibility.

The Indiana Pacers selected him 10th overall, making him the top NBA draft selection in Fresno State history. 

Furthermore, he signed his rookie contract with the Pacers on July 1, 2010. The agreement was a two-year, $3.9 million deferred deal with team options.

NBA Career

On October 27, George finally made his NBA debut.

His debut game was against the San Antonio Spurs, playing 23 minutes and scoring four points. 

In the 2011 NBA playoffs, he was one of two rookies from the 2010 NBA Draft who started for his side. 

George was entitled to the 2011 NBA All-Rookie Second Team at the end of the season.

George started each of the 66 games he appeared in during the season. In the 2012–2013 season, he was named NBA Player of the Week for the first time in his career.

He also had a strong playoff run, posting his first career playoff triple-double. 

However, he was selected to the NBA’s Second All-Defensive Team. The player was recruited to the All-NBA Third Team after his postseason Campaign.

Los Angeles Clippers (2019–present)

George started his affiliation with the Los Angeles Clippers on July 20, 2019.

However, he made his Clippers debut on November 14, 2019, scoring 33 points in a 132–127 defeat to the New Orleans Pelicans.
