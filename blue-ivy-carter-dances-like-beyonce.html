Blue Ivy Carter Channels Mom Beyonc's Dance Moves in New Video

  • In a new clip shared by her personal stylist, Blue Ivy Carter is seen dancing along to her mother Beyoncé's song "Mood 4 Eva" from The Lion King: The Gift album.
  • The eight-year-old donned a dusty-pink jumpsuit, brown lipstick, and chic cornrows for the quick clip showcasing her dance moves.
  • The snippet is from Beyoncé's 2019 Making the Gift documentary, which revealed the singer's creative process behind making the Lion King-inspired album.

What can't Blue Ivy Carter do?

In recent months, the eight-year-old daughter of Beyoncé and Jay-Z has proved herself to be a budding style icon, a science aficionado, an NAACP award winner, and now a very promising candidate to become Beyoncé's next dance captain.

In a new video shared by Blue Ivy's own personal stylist, Manuel A. Mendez (yes, she has her own personal stylist!), Blue can be seen performing very impressive choreography to Beyoncé's "Mood 4 Eva," a song from her recent musical project, The Lion King: The Gift. In the clip, Blue dons a dusty-rose pink jumpsuit and dark brown lipstick, and wears her hair in sleek cornrows as she dances along to her mother's song.

"Mood 4 Eva. #love," Mendez captioned the video of his young client.

One can't help but take note of how much Blue emulates her mom in the clip, from her dramatic arm extensions to her facial expressions, and even her hair and makeup.

Beyhive fans have been lucky enough to receive more than a few glances of Blue via social media in recent weeks, including over the weekend, when she apparently made a surprise vocal cameo in her grandmother Tina Knowles-Lawson's Mother's Day Instagram post.

"Happy Mother's Day to all of the beautiful mothers out there," Knowles-Lawson says in the clip before Blue seemingly chimes in.

"And don't forget all the beautiful daughters out there, which I am one of," she supposedly says in the video.

And that she is!
