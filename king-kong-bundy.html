King Kong Bundy Height, Weight, Age, Biography, Wife, Kids &amp; Family

King Kong Bundy (born November 7, 1957 – died March 4, 2019) was an American Former Professional Wrestler and Television Personality from Atlantic City, New Jersey. He gained fame after his appearances in the World Wrestling Federation (WWF). Furthermore, the 61-year-old wrestler made his debut in the wrestling world on March 7, 1981.

But he became popular in the mid-1980s and mid-1990s. He also developed his signature move named “the five count“. Moreover, his real full birth name is Christopher Alan Pallies. In his career, Bundy remained a two-time NWA American Heavyweight and Tag Team Champion.

In addition, Christopher wrestled against the legend Hulk Hogan in a steel cage match for the WWF World Heavyweight Championship. The competition took place in the main event of WrestleMania 2 in 1986.

Recently, on March 4, 2019, the former wrestler died at the age of 61 due to Complications of diabetes. The sad news was announced by his longtime friend and manager “David Herro” on Facebook.

In fact, many celebrities from the wrestling industry and fans also shared their memories of King Kong Bundy on social media. Mr. Hulk Hogan showed his grief at the death of his former rival and he was overwhelmed by the sad news.
