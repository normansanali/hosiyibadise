Lilia Vu Boyfriend: Is Lilia Vu Dating?

Lilia Vu boyfriend-American professional golfer, Lilia Kha-Tu Du Vu was born on October 14th, 1997 in Fountain Valley, California in the United States of America.

Vu was born to Kieu Thuy and Douglas Vu. She shares the same parents with her brother, Andre Vu who played on the UC Riverside golf team.

She stands at a height of 1.65m tall and weighs 69kg. Vu attended the University of California, Los Angeles.

Lilia Vu career

For the U.S. National Team, Vu made a number of successful performances. She earned four points at the 2018 Curtis Cup and, along with Jennifer Kupcho and Kristen Gillman, won the 2018 Espirito Santo Trophy in Ireland.

She also triumphed in the 2018 Arnold Palmer Cup at the French golf course at Evian Resort. She finished second behind Lauren Stephenson in the 2014 AJGA Girls Championship, and she won the 2016 Women’s Southern California Amateur Championship.

She tied for third at the Canadian Women’s Amateur and finished third alone at the SCGA Women’s Amateur Championship.

2015 saw Vu enroll at UCLA. She won the Pac-12 Conference Freshman of the Year honor in 2016 while a UCLA Bruin, then in 2018 she finished as a finalist for the Honda Trophy and was named WGCA Player of the Year and Pac-12 Conference Golfer of the Year.

She was also named to the All-Pac 12 team three times and the First Team of the WGCA, and she holds the record for the most victories in UCLA history with eight individual titles.

Vu participated in the ANA Inspiration and the U.S. Women’s Open as an amateur. At the 2018 ANA Inspiration, she had the lowest amateur score with 285 (-3).

In 2018 and 2019, she held the top spot in the World Amateur Golf Ranking for a total of 31 weeks. As she went professional in January 2019, her era came to an end.

In order to gain entry for the 2019 LPGA Tour, Vu, who turned professional in January 2019, had to place T27 in the inaugural LPGA Q-Series. In her nine starts on the LPGA Tour, she only managed to make one cut.

She advanced into the top 250 of the Women’s World Golf Rankings in 2021 after winning three championships on the Symetra Tour.

She also took home the 2021 Potawatomi Cup in addition to winning the Garden City Charity Classic, Twin Bridges Championship, and Four Winds Invitational.

She won the Symetra Tour Player of the Year award, placed first on the money list for the season, and was given a card for the LPGA Tour in 2022.
